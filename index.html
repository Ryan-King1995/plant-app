Outdoor Plant Collection

Asiatic Lily

Location: Well‑drained, moist soil enriched with organic matter; prefers alkaline conditions. Plant bulbs about 15 cm deep in autumn with the base of the plant in shade and the top growth in full sun.

Care: Provide at least half a day of sunlight. Water during dry spells but avoid waterlogging. Deadhead spent flowers to tidy the plant.

Post‑season: Deadhead by trimming faded flowerheads just above a pair of leaves; leave the stems and leaves to die back naturally, then cut them down when yellow.

Oriental Lily

Location: Moist but well‑drained acidic soil in a sunny spot; plant bulbs deep (around 15 cm) with good drainage.

Care: Keep soil evenly moist but not soggy. Stake tall stems if necessary and deadhead after flowering.

Post‑season: Deadhead by trimming spent flowerheads above a pair of leaves and allow the stems to die back; remove them once yellow.

Golden Bamboo

Location: Sheltered site in sun or light shade with moist, fertile, free‑draining soil; bamboos tolerate most soils but dislike waterlogged or extremely dry conditions.

Care: Plant in spring; keep the soil consistently moist, especially while establishing. Space clumps 1–1.5 m apart and feed with nitrogen‑rich compost in spring and midsummer.

Post‑season: In spring, remove dead or weak canes at ground level; thin congested clumps and trim the tops if needed to control height and encourage fresh foliage.

Leucanthemum ‘Broadway Lights’

Location: Moderately fertile, moist but well‑drained soil in full sun or partial shade.

Care: Stake plants if needed, deadhead regularly and cut stems down to ground level in winter.

Post‑season: During summer continue to remove faded flower stems, and in autumn or early spring cut back dead growth to the ground.

Crocosmia ‘George Davison’

Location: Medium moisture, moderately fertile, well‑drained soil in full sun to part shade; full sun is preferred in cool climates.

Care: Plant corms 7–10 cm deep after the last frost; water to establish but allow the soil to dry slightly between waterings. Protect corms in cold regions or lift and store them over winter.

Post‑season: Allow foliage to die back naturally and cut it down in late autumn or early spring to tidy the clump.

Lobelia ‘Starship Scarlet’

Location: Rich, moist to wet soil in full sun to part shade; tolerates brief flooding but soil must never dry out.

Care: Keep soil constantly moist. Provide afternoon shade in hot climates and mulch the roots in cold regions to protect them.

Post‑season: For perennial lobelias, deadhead after flowering and cut back foliage in autumn; mulch to protect the crown over winter. Annual types can be cut back in mid‑summer to maintain bushiness.

Climbing Rose ‘Chawton Cottage’

Location: Fertile, humus‑rich, moist but well‑drained soil in full sun.

Care: Apply a balanced fertiliser and mulch in late winter or early spring and again in early summer. Tie new canes into supports and deadhead after the first flush to encourage reblooming.

Post‑season: Deadhead spent blooms throughout the season. In winter, prune side shoots back to four healthy buds and tie in long stems, cutting just above outward‑facing buds for good shape.

Clematis

Location: Fertile, moisture‑retentive soil with good drainage. Keep the roots cool and shaded while allowing the top growth to reach the sun.

Care: Water regularly and feed weekly in summer with a high‑potash fertiliser. Mulch or place stones over the root area to keep it cool. Prune according to the plant’s pruning group.

Post‑season: Pruning depends on the clematis group: early‑flowering types are lightly trimmed after flowering; large‑flowered hybrids are pruned in late winter and again after the first flush; late‑flowering varieties are cut back hard in late winter.

Camellia ‘Mary Williams’

Location: Acidic, humus‑rich soil in a woodland setting. Choose a site sheltered from cold, dry winds and early morning sun.

Care: Water during dry spells and mulch to conserve moisture. Lightly prune after flowering (pruning group 8) and protect buds from frost.

Post‑season: Carry out major pruning just after flowering or in late February; severe cuts removing up to one‑third of the plant help rejuvenate but may reduce next season’s blooms.

Dianthus (Cottage Pink)

Location: Rich, well‑drained soil kept moist but not soggy; avoid organic mulches that cause rot.

Care: Dress with compost and balanced fertiliser in spring. Water weekly or more in hot weather and deadhead to prolong flowering. Divide clumps if growth thins.

Post‑season: Continue deadheading throughout the season; shear the plant lightly after the main flush of flowers and cut back stems later in the season to tidy.

Lavatera

Location: Sunny site with well‑draining soil. Tolerates drier soils and clay but dislikes wet winter conditions.

Care: Once established, lavateras are fairly drought tolerant and rarely need fertiliser. Lightly tidy plants in autumn and cut back hard in late spring for sub‑shrubs; herbaceous species are cut down in winter.

Post‑season: After flowering, tidy plants. In late spring cut sub‑shrubs back to strong shoots; herbaceous lavateras should be cut to the ground in winter.

Heuchera ‘Paris’

Location: Humus‑rich, fertile, well‑draining soil with a pH around 5.5–6.2. Thrives in part shade or dappled shade; avoid hot afternoon sun.

Care: Water regularly, particularly when grown in full sun. A light compost mulch is appreciated but avoid heavy fertiliser. Deadhead to extend blooming, cut stems back after flowering and divide clumps every 3–4 years.

Post‑season: Remove spent flower spikes and tidy leaves; cut back stems after flowering and divide clumps every few years to maintain vigour.

Catananche caerulea

Location: Full sun and well‑drained, light/sandy soil with good drought resistance. The plant is best in a mixed border or gravel garden.

Care: Water sparingly once established; the plant may be short‑lived on heavy soils. Group three or five plants for a display, cut back after flowering and divide in spring if needed.

Post‑season: Cut flower stems back to the base after flowering. Divide congested clumps in spring to renew the plant.

Hardy Fuchsia ‘Hawkeshead’

Location: Rich, well‑drained soil with consistent moisture; grows in full sun with regular water or in partial shade.

Care: Plant in early summer and water regularly during dry spells. Plant the base 5 cm deeper for winter protection, prune dead shoots in spring and mulch after pruning. Plants die back below −6 °C but reshoot in spring.

Post‑season: Deadhead flowers through the season. In spring, cut away dead shoots to the base and apply mulch to protect the crown.

Kniphofia ‘Pyromania Backdraft’

Location: Any well‑drained soil with a slightly acidic pH (around 5.8–6.2). Prefers full sun and high light levels.

Care: Water moderately to keep the soil moist, giving extra water when blooming. Provide winter mulch in cold zones and cut back leaves in spring to encourage new growth.

Post‑season: Remove spent flower stems and, in early spring, cut back foliage to near ground level; apply mulch for winter protection.

Acer ‘Atropurpureum’

Location: Slightly acidic, humus‑rich, well‑drained soil. Variegated forms prefer partial shade; green leaves tolerate more sun, while red and purple cultivars need some sun for their colour.

Care: Plant at the same depth as in the pot and avoid covering the roots deeply; water regularly during the first year and during dry spells. Mulch annually and protect leaves from scorching afternoon sun and cold winds.

Post‑season: Japanese maples require minimal pruning; remove only dead, damaged or crossing branches in late autumn or winter and avoid heavy cuts.

Indoor Plant Collection

Monstera deliciosa

Location: Bright but filtered light and warm temperatures (18–25°C). Use an open, well‑drained slightly acidic compost and provide a support pole.

Care: Water when the compost is almost dry, using rainwater or filtered water. Mist the foliage or stand the pot on a tray of damp pebbles to raise humidity. Feed monthly from spring to autumn and wipe leaves to remove dust.

Post‑season: Remove yellow or damaged leaves and prune back long vines in spring. Repot when roots fill the pot.

Areca palm

Location: Bright, indirect light in a warm room (18–24°C) away from draughts. Grow in a soil‑based, well‑draining compost and keep the plant snug in its pot.

Care: Water thoroughly when the top few centimetres of compost dry out, using rainwater if possible. Mist leaves or place the pot on a tray of moist pebbles to increase humidity. Feed every couple of months during the growing season.

Post‑season: Trim brown or yellowing fronds and repot in spring only when the roots fill the pot.

Lipstick plant

Location: Bright, indirect light with a few hours of winter sun. Provide high humidity and a loose, slightly acidic, well‑draining compost similar to orchid mix.

Care: Keep the soil evenly moist, allowing the top couple of centimetres to dry out between waterings. Water from the bottom with lukewarm, low‑lime water and remove excess after 15 minutes. Feed every three to four weeks in spring and summer. Mist regularly or use a pebble tray to maintain humidity.

Post‑season: Remove faded stems and cut back long shoots in spring to maintain shape. Repot only every few years to avoid disturbing flower buds.

Mistletoe cactus

Location: Bright, indirect light; tolerates a little morning or evening sun. Grow in a peat‑free cactus compost and keep slightly pot‑bound.

Care: Water more frequently than desert cacti: in bright light, water thoroughly and allow the top of the compost to dry between waterings; in shade, keep the compost just damp. Reduce watering in winter. Average room humidity is fine, but mist the plant when the air is dry.

Post‑season: Pruning is rarely needed; trim back any overly long stems to maintain the plant’s shape. Repot every three to four years.

Striped Calathea

Location: Bright but indirect light; north‑ or east‑facing windows in summer and a brighter spot in winter. Keep the plant warm (16–21°C) and away from draughts.

Care: Maintain high humidity by misting or standing the pot on a tray of damp gravel. Water moderately in spring and summer to keep the compost evenly moist; allow the surface to dry slightly in autumn and winter. Use rainwater or filtered water and feed monthly from April to September.

Post‑season: Remove any brown or damaged leaves and divide the plant in spring if it becomes congested. Repot when necessary.

English ivy

Location: Medium to bright indirect light in a cool spot (10–21°C). Avoid direct sun and dry air.

Care: Water when the top centimetre of compost dries out and allow excess water to drain. Mist regularly or stand the pot on pebbles in water to increase humidity. Feed lightly during the growing season.

Post‑season: Trim any long or straggly stems to keep the plant tidy and remove discoloured leaves. Repot or refresh the compost every couple of years.

Dieffenbachia compacta

Location: Bright, indirect light; avoid direct sun. Use a fertile, well‑draining potting mix rich in peat or coir and keep away from cold drafts.

Care: Water when the top two centimetres of soil feel dry, providing regular moisture but never allowing the soil to become waterlogged. Average household temperatures and humidity are sufficient, although slightly higher humidity is appreciated. Feed every four to six weeks in the growing season.

Post‑season: Remove yellowing leaves and wipe dust from foliage. Repot when the plant outgrows its pot and trim tall stems to encourage bushier growth.
